SaaS AI Disruption Index

How vulnerable are major SaaS companies to AI disruption? A composite score based on end-user domain expertise, customer size distribution, and standard-setter defensibility.

Methodology

Three Disruption Factors (1-10 scale)

Domain Expertise Overlap (45% weight): How much domain expertise do end users have in the area the software serves? Higher overlap means users can more easily build or adopt AI alternatives. Example: developers using Atlassian can vibe-code replacements.

Small Business Exposure (30% weight): Does the company primarily serve SMBs or enterprises? SMB-heavy companies face higher disruption risk as small businesses are more willing to switch to cheaper AI-native tools.

Standard-Setter Defensibility (25% weight, inverted): Is the company a de facto standard in its domain? Standard setters (Adobe, Salesforce) have stronger moats. This factor is inverted in the composite: high defensibility reduces disruption risk.

Composite Score

Disruption Score = (Domain Expertise * 0.45) + (SMB Exposure * 0.30) + ((10 - Defensibility) * 0.25)

Higher score = more vulnerable to AI disruption. The X-axis shows domain expertise overlap, Y-axis shows SMB exposure, dot color indicates defensibility, and dot size reflects the composite disruption score.
